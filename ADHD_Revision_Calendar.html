Phase Breakdown

Your exams span 21 days with gaps between each. The strategy: learn every topic during Phase 1, hit past papers in Phase 2, then use the gaps between exams for laser-focused sprints. IY3612 and IY3660 get 5-10% extra coverage through dedicated work-day evening reviews and extra past papers — they're first AND hardest. Mondays and Fridays are kept light (work days) with optional evening flashcard review only.
PHASE 1

Foundation (Mar 24 – ~Apr 29) — All 62 Topics

Every topic across all 6 modules is introduced, 3 per study day (Tue/Wed/Thu/Sat). Topics are interleaved — never the same module twice in one day. Work-day evenings alternate between IY3612 and IY3660 flashcard review for the extra 5-10% focus.

PHASE 2

Past Papers (~Apr 30 – May 4)

Timed past papers under exam conditions. IY3612 and IY3660 each get 2 papers (others get 1). Mark your own work. Identify weak spots for the sprint phase.

PHASE 3

Pre-Exam Sprints (May 5 – 28)

Each gap between exams is dedicated to the NEXT exam. Flashcard blitz + final past paper + weak-area cramming. After each exam, immediately switch focus to the next one.

ADHD Study Techniques

Blurting

The Blurting Method

Read a topic for 10 min. Close everything. Write EVERYTHING you remember. Open notes — different colour for gaps. The gaps are what to focus on next.

Flashcards

Active Recall Cards

Anki or physical cards. Front: question. Back: answer. Rate confidence 1-5. Wrong cards return sooner. Essential for all security definitions and crypto formulas.

Teach It

Feynman Technique

Explain a concept out loud as if teaching a friend. If you stumble, you've found a gap. Great for IY3612 — practice saying "brief, reasoned answers" aloud.

Past Papers

Exam Simulation

Timed papers under exam conditions (2hrs). Mark yourself. Single best predictor of exam performance.

Your Ideal Study Day (ADHD-Optimised)

Each study day has 3 Pomodoro blocks (25+5 min = 90 min total). The blocks follow Warm-up → Peak Focus → Cool-down. Each block uses a different module to maintain novelty.
08:00

Morning Routine

Wake, shower, breakfast, coffee. No screens for 15 min — protects dopamine for study.

09:00

BLOCK 1 — Warm-Up (25 min)

Start with something accessible. A lighter module, a review, an interesting new topic. Builds momentum and overcomes start-up resistance.

09:30

Short Break (5 min)

Quick stretch, water, look away from screen. Stay near your desk.

09:35

BLOCK 2 — Peak Focus (25 min)

Hardest topic: crypto formulas, graph theory, RSA proofs. Use active recall: close notes, write from memory, check gaps.

10:05

Big Break (20 min)

Walk, snack, stretch. Leave your desk completely. Movement resets focus.

10:25

BLOCK 3 — Cool-Down (25 min)

Spaced repetition review or lighter new topic from a third module. Dopamine reward from recalling something you know.

11:00

Lunch + Life Admin

Proper meal. One chore. Staying on top of life prevents stress spirals.

12:30

OPTIONAL: Afternoon Session

If energy is good, do another 3-block cycle with different modules. Max 2 sessions/day (3 hrs total). Consistency beats intensity.

14:00+

Free Time

Exercise, gaming, socialising, groceries, cooking. Brain needs rest to consolidate.
